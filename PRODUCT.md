# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Makers who need to collect structured input — survey creators, small teams, event organizers, founders, ops people — who currently reach for Google Forms or Microsoft Forms and find the manual field-by-field building tedious. Their context: they have an idea for a form and want it built and shareable in under a minute, often as a side task inside a larger workflow. They are not designers; they want the structure handled for them and a clean, trustworthy public form to send out.

## Product Purpose

FormAI turns a natural-language prompt into an editable, publishable form. Describe the form, let the LLM draft the structure (question types, required fields, options), fine-tune in a drag-and-drop builder, publish a public link, and review responses from a dashboard. Success looks like: idea → published form in well under a minute, with respondents filling it out on a surface that feels more modern and trustworthy than the incumbent form tools.

## Brand Personality

Fast and confident. Sharp, efficient, gets out of the way. Speed and capability are the promise — the product should feel like it already knows what you want and hands it back instantly. Voice is direct and plain, never cute or over-explained. Quiet competence over loud marketing.

## Anti-references

- **Google Forms / Microsoft Forms**: the dated, utilitarian, generic enterprise form tools FormAI exists to replace. Nothing here should read as a beige settings panel, a radio-button wall, or a clip-art header.

## Design Principles

- **Instant, not impressive**: the win is speed and certainty. Favor flows that get the user to a published form fast over flourishes that slow them down.
- **The structure is the product**: the AI doing the field-by-field work is the core value. Surface generation, editing, and reordering as confident, legible, low-friction interactions.
- **Trustworthy in public**: the respondent-facing form is the surface strangers judge. It must feel clean, credible, and effortless to fill — that's the brand's reputation in the wild.
- **Quiet confidence**: dark, focused UI with one decisive accent. Restraint over decoration; let capability speak.
- **Consistent weight**: the same conceptual action looks the same everywhere — primary actions stay primary across dashboard, builder, and public form.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 against its (dark) background — the dark theme makes muted slate text on slate surfaces the primary contrast risk; keep body copy toward the light end of the ramp. Visible keyboard focus on every interactive element. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Public form must be fully keyboard- and screen-reader-navigable, since respondents arrive with unknown needs and no onboarding.
